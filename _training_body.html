What You'll Install

Three Layers. One Mission.

A church doesn't get healthy one layer at a time. Over two days you'll work all three together, because fixing one while ignoring the others is exactly how churches end up with passionate chaos or efficient sterility.

01

The System

Structure that holds the weight of the vision. Define the ministry system so your church can invest in the right people, stop re-solving the same problems, delegate with confidence, and stay focused on what moves the mission.

02

The Soul

Identity that makes people want to be part of it. Nothing creates lasting ministry advantage like a church rooted in its calling, culture, and core values. When your team knows who you are, every decision gets cleaner.

03

The Integration

The part everyone skips. Systems that don't connect to the soul get abandoned, and culture without structure evaporates. You'll leave with the operating rhythm that makes both stick after you go home.

Why This Is Different

Most Operating Systems Were Built For Businesses. A Church Is Not A Business.

Business frameworks can help your team run better. But your volunteers aren't employees, your congregation isn't a customer base, and your mission isn't a revenue target. Here's the honest comparison.

A Business Operating System

Aligns the leadership team around operational goals. Efficient, but it treats the church like a company and quietly starves the soul. Works for about 18 months, then the culture pushes back.

The Church Accelerator Framework

Drives performance and develops people, at the same time. Built on the System & Soul framework and adapted for the local church, so structure and culture grow together. You own the tools when you leave.

A Conference Or Consultant

Shows up, says something valuable, and leaves. You get inspired for two weeks and a three-ring binder for the shelf. Nothing is installed, so nothing actually changes.

Common Questions

Before You Register

I'm not technically an executive pastor. Should I still come?

If you carry the operational weight of the church and have real authority to change how it runs, you're in the right room regardless of what your title says. Plenty of churches call this role something else. If you can't influence structural decisions, though, this training will frustrate you more than help you.

Should my lead pastor come with me?

This room is deliberately built for executive pastors, and there's real value in that. You'll work through problems candidly with people in your exact seat. If your lead pastor wants in on the work, the full 12-month Church Accelerator Framework engagement is designed for that, and it brings your whole team in.

What size church is this for?

The 5 Rights System™ works in a church of 150 or a church of 10,000, because it's built on how people grow spiritually, which doesn't change with attendance. What changes is the complexity of the systems. Gavin has worked with churches under 200 and over 8,000.

How much prep is required?

You'll get a short pre-work packet about two weeks out. Expect roughly an hour. It matters, because the two days are spent building on your actual church, not on a hypothetical one.

What happens after the training?

You leave with documented systems and a 90-day plan you own. Many executive pastors go home and run it themselves. Others bring their lead pastor into a conversation about the full Church Accelerator Framework engagement. Both are good outcomes and there's no pressure toward either.

What's included in the price?

[[TRAINING_PRICE]] covers both full days, all materials and frameworks, meals during the training, and your documented deliverables. Travel and lodging are on you.
